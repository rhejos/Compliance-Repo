## Update 1: Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA)

**Effective Date:** July 1, 2024

**Summary:**
Texas has enacted a comprehensive consumer privacy law similar to the Virginia CDPA. It applies to businesses that:
- Conduct business in Texas OR produce products/services targeted to Texas residents
- Process personal data of 100,000+ Texas consumers annually, OR
- Process personal data of 25,000+ consumers and derive 50%+ revenue from selling data

**Key Requirements:**
- Consumer rights: access, correction, deletion, data portability, opt-out
- Privacy notices with specific disclosures
- Data protection assessments for high-risk processing
- Opt-out mechanisms for targeted advertising and sales
- Reasonable security measures

**Penalties:**
- Up to $7,500 per violation
- 30-day cure period for first violations
- Texas AG enforcement only (no private right of action)

## Update 2: EU AI Act - High-Risk AI Systems

**Effective Date:** August 2025 (phased implementation through 2027)

**Summary:**
The EU AI Act creates a regulatory framework for artificial intelligence. It categorizes AI systems by risk level and imposes requirements on high-risk AI systems.

**Key Requirements for High-Risk AI:**
- Risk management system
- Data governance and quality requirements
- Technical documentation
- Record-keeping and logging
- Transparency and user information
- Human oversight capabilities
- Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity

**Scope:**
Applies to providers of AI systems placed on the EU market or put into service in the EU, regardless of where the provider is located.

**Penalties:**
- Up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover for prohibited practices
- Up to €15 million or 3% for other violations

## Update 3: PCI-DSS 4.0 Compliance Deadline

**Effective Date:** March 31, 2025 (all 4.0 requirements become mandatory)

**Summary:**
PCI-DSS 4.0 was released in March 2022 with a transition period. As of March 31, 2024, version 3.2.1 retired. By March 31, 2025, all "future-dated" requirements in 4.0 become mandatory.

**Key Changes from 3.2.1:**
- Customized approach option (alternative to defined approach)
- Enhanced authentication requirements (MFA for all CDE access)
- New e-commerce requirements (payment page script management)
- Targeted risk analysis for flexible control implementation
- Enhanced security awareness training

**Applicability:**
Any organization that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data.

**Penalties:**
- Fines from $5,000 to $100,000+ per month
- Potential loss of ability to process card payments

## Update 4: HIPAA Security Rule Update Proposal

**Status:** Proposed Rule (not yet final)
**Expected Effective Date:** TBD (likely 12-18 months after finalization)

**Summary:**
HHS has proposed significant updates to the HIPAA Security Rule for the first time since 2013. The proposed rule would:
- Remove distinction between "required" and "addressable" specifications
- Require encryption of ePHI at rest and in transit (with limited exceptions)
- Mandate multi-factor authentication
- Require vulnerability scanning every 6 months and penetration testing annually
- Mandate 24-hour notification to HHS of restoration from backup

**Applicability:**
HIPAA covered entities and business associates handling ePHI.

**Context:**
This is a proposed rule open for public comment. Final requirements may change.

## Update 5: New York DFS Cybersecurity Regulation Amendment

**Effective Date:** November 2023 (some requirements phased to 2025)

**Summary:**
NY DFS has amended 23 NYCRR 500 with enhanced cybersecurity requirements for covered financial services companies.

**Key Changes:**
- New "Class A" company category for large entities (2,000+ employees or $1B+ revenue)
- Enhanced access controls and MFA requirements
- New business continuity and disaster recovery requirements
- Independent audits required for Class A companies
- Enhanced incident reporting (24-hour notification for certain events)

**Applicability:**
Licensed financial services companies operating in New York (banks, insurance companies, mortgage servicers, etc.)

**Penalties:**
- Significant financial penalties
- Potential license revocation

## Update 6: California Delete Act (SB 362)

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026 (deletion mechanism launch)

**Summary:**
California's Delete Act requires data brokers to register with the California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) and participate in an accessible deletion mechanism that will allow consumers to submit a single request to delete their information from all registered data brokers.

**Key Requirements:**
- Data broker registration ($400 annual fee)
- Participation in one-stop deletion mechanism
- Delete personal information within 45 days of verified request
- Audit requirements every 3 years

**Definition of Data Broker:**
A business that knowingly collects and sells to third parties the personal information of consumers with whom the business does not have a direct relationship.

**Penalties:**
- $200 per day for failure to register
- Additional penalties for violations

## Update 7: SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules

**Effective Date:** December 2023 (for large companies); June 2024 (for smaller companies)

**Summary:**
The SEC adopted rules requiring public companies to disclose:
- Material cybersecurity incidents within 4 business days (Form 8-K)
- Annual disclosure of cybersecurity risk management and governance (Form 10-K)

**Key Requirements:**
- Material incident disclosure: nature, scope, timing, material impact
- Annual disclosure of cybersecurity risk management processes
- Board oversight of cybersecurity risks
- Management's role in assessing and managing cyber risk

**Applicability:**
Publicly traded companies registered with the SEC.

**Penalties:**
- SEC enforcement actions
- Securities fraud liability
- Shareholder lawsuits

## Update 8: UK Data Protection and Digital Information Bill

**Status:** Pending (expected to pass)
**Expected Effective Date:** Late 2024 or 2025

**Summary:**
The UK is reforming its data protection framework post-Brexit. The bill would:
- Reduce documentation requirements for lower-risk processing
- Reform cookie consent requirements
- Modify data subject access request rules
- Create new "recognized legitimate interests" category
- Reform international transfer mechanisms

**Key Changes:**
- Some reduction in compliance burden vs. current UK GDPR
- Changes to lawful basis documentation
- Modified consent requirements for cookies
- New rules for automated decision-making

**Applicability:**
Organizations processing personal data of UK residents.
